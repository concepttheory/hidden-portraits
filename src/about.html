Hidden portraits

by Volker Hermes

Ten years in the making, Hidden Portraits reinterprets historical paintings through a modern lens.

German artist Volker Hermes’s visual manipulation of Old Masters paintings and sculptures explore the viewers' response to portraits where the face is obscured.

By blocking the focus on the emotions expressed by the face, viewers become free to shift their focus to the codes of fashion as symbols of self-representation and social status. Then, with a contemporary perspective, great respect, and a dash of humor, Hermes layers codes of our times over the codes of ancient times.

Ten years in the making, Hidden Portraits reinterprets historical paintings through a modern lens. Using only elements from the original work, German artist Volker Hermes visually manipulates famous Old Masters portraits, exploring expression when the face is obscured.

By blocking the emotions expressed by the face, Hermes invites viewers to observe ancient codes of fashion as symbols of self-representation and social status. Then, with a contemporary perspective, great respect, and a dash of humor, Hermes layers the codes of our times over the codes of ancient times.
